**Management Insights**

- **Revenue Recognition:** The company has adopted new accounting standards related to revenue recognition, which had a material impact on its financial statements.

- **Impairment of Investment Securities:** The company reviews investments quarterly for indicators of impairment. In making this judgment, it employs a systematic methodology that considers both quantitative and qualitative evidence.

- **Goodwill:** The company tests goodwill for impairment annually. The fair value of each reporting unit is estimated primarily through a discounted cash flow methodology, which requires significant judgments and assumptions.

- **Research and Development Costs:** The company capitalizes software development costs until the product is available for general release to customers. It determines technological feasibility before capitalizing these costs.

- **Legal and Other Contingencies:** The company accretes an estimated loss from a loss contingency if it is probable that an asset has been impaired or a liability has been incurred. Changes in the factors considered in this assessment could materially impact the financial statements.

- **Income Taxes:** The company recognizes the tax benefit from an uncertain tax position only if it is more likely than not that the position will be sustained. Variations in the actual outcome of these future tax consequences could materially impact the financial statements.

- **Inventories:** The company reviews inventory quantities, purchase commitments, and estimated utility on a regular basis. If its review indicates a reduction in utility, it reduces inventory to a new cost basis.
